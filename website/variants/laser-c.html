The Treatment

What Is Laser Hair Removal?

Laser hair removal is a clinically proven method of permanently reducing unwanted hair. A concentrated beam of light targets the melanin pigment inside each hair follicle, heating it to a temperature that damages the follicle and prevents future growth — without harming the surrounding skin.

Unlike shaving or waxing, which only offer temporary results, laser treatment works beneath the surface to disrupt the hair growth cycle at its root. Over a course of sessions, the hair becomes finer, lighter and eventually stops growing back altogether.

Three Wavelengths

Tailored to Your Skin

At MARR, we use three laser wavelengths — Alexandrite 755nm, Diode 808nm and Nd:YAG 1064nm — so we can safely and effectively treat all skin types and hair colours.

Each wavelength is chosen for its specific strengths, allowing us to customise every treatment to your individual needs. This multi-wavelength approach means no one is turned away — we have the right laser for every client.

The safest wavelength for darker and deeply pigmented skin types. Its longer wavelength bypasses surface melanin to target the follicle directly, reducing the risk of hyperpigmentation.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is laser hair removal painful?

Most clients describe the sensation as a quick snap or warm flick against the skin — uncomfortable for a moment but very manageable. The Alexandrite laser has a built-in cooling system that numbs the skin as it fires, making treatments far more comfortable than waxing. Sensitivity varies by treatment area, but the majority of our clients are pleasantly surprised by how tolerable it is.

How many sessions will I need?

Most clients need between 6 and 8 sessions to achieve significant permanent hair reduction. This is because hair grows in cycles and the laser can only target follicles during the active growth phase. Sessions are typically spaced 4 to 8 weeks apart depending on the body area. After completing a course, some clients opt for occasional maintenance sessions once or twice a year.

What skin types can be treated?

We can safely treat all skin types, from very fair (Fitzpatrick I) through to deeply pigmented skin (Fitzpatrick VI). Our clinic uses three laser wavelengths — Alexandrite 755nm, Diode 808nm and Nd:YAG 1064nm — which means we can select the safest and most effective laser for your individual skin tone and hair type. During your consultation, we will assess your skin and recommend the best approach.

Is there any downtime?

There is no downtime. You can return to normal activities straight away. Immediately after treatment you may notice mild redness or slight swelling around the hair follicles, similar to a mild sunburn. This typically settles within a few hours. We recommend avoiding direct sun exposure, hot baths and vigorous exercise for 24 to 48 hours after each session.

Is it permanent?

Laser hair removal delivers permanent hair reduction, which means the treated hair follicles are permanently damaged and will not regrow. However, hormonal changes, medication or previously dormant follicles can sometimes stimulate new growth in the same area. This is why we refer to the treatment as permanent reduction rather than permanent removal. Most clients enjoy years of smooth skin with only occasional touch-up sessions.
